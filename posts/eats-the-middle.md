AI is compressing the cost of execution across every value chain. Building software. Producing creative. Drafting documents. Writing code. Generating analysis. The middle of every business — the actual making of the thing — is getting cheaper, faster, and more commoditised by the month.

When the middle commoditises, value pools at the ends. Two moats survive. The why, on one end. Distribution, on the other. Everything in between is borrowed time. Most companies are still investing in the middle because the middle is where the org chart sits, and they have not yet noticed that the part of the org chart they keep adding to is the part AI is busy hollowing out.

### The value chain, drawn

Every business runs a value chain that turns an idea into something a customer pays for. A simplified version:

```
   THE WHY              THE MIDDLE              DISTRIBUTION
   ────────             ──────────              ────────────
   judgment             execution               channel
   taste                building                relationships
   articulation         shipping                trust with audience
   vision               operations              demand capture

   ┌─────────┐  ┌──────────────────────┐  ┌─────────────┐
   │         │  │                      │  │             │
   │  WHY    │──│      MIDDLE          │──│DISTRIBUTION │
   │         │  │                      │  │             │
   └─────────┘  └──────────────────────┘  └─────────────┘
       ↑                  ↓                       ↑
   compounds         commoditising              compounds
       │              fast (AI)                    │
       │                                           │
       └─────── value pools at the ends ───────────┘
```

The "why" end is the part that decides what should exist and articulates why anyone should care. Taste, judgment, point of view, the resonance of a value proposition. Design and marketing in the broad sense — the disciplines that turn a thing into something people understand and want.

The "distribution" end is the path from a finished thing to a person who pays for it. Channel ownership. Audience trust. Sales motion. The relationships and habits that put the thing in front of the person at the moment they are ready to buy.

The middle is everything between the two. The actual making. Writing the code. Producing the asset. Running the operation. Translating the spec into the artifact.

### Why the middle is on fire

AI is compressing the cost of the middle to near zero, asymmetrically and unevenly, but undeniably. Code generation. Content generation. Operational tooling. Customer service. Internal analytics. Each of these is a job that used to require a layer of people, and increasingly requires one person and a model. The cost curve on building things is collapsing faster than any team is keeping up with.

This does not mean the middle disappears. It means the middle stops being a moat. A capability that everyone has at near-zero marginal cost is, by definition, not a competitive advantage. It is table stakes.

> A capability that everyone has at near-zero marginal cost is not a moat. It is table stakes.

### Why the why end compounds

The why end does not commoditise because it requires a point of view. AI reverts to the mean. Articulating a value proposition that resonates, spotting what should exist before anyone else does, holding the taste to know what is worth keeping — these are the things AI raises the floor on but cannot substitute for. They depend on judgment that has been calibrated against specific customers, specific markets, specific moments in time, and that calibration is what makes a why durable.

The companies with the strongest "why" position are the ones where someone — usually a founder, sometimes a designer or marketer with real authority — has spent enough time inside the problem to hold a sharper view of it than the market does. That sharpness compounds. The next decision they make is informed by the last hundred. AI does not have those hundred. It has averages.

### Why distribution compounds

Distribution does not commoditise because it depends on relationships, trust, and channel ownership that accumulate over years. You can prompt your way to a landing page in an hour. You cannot prompt your way to an audience that trusts you, a sales motion that works, or a channel where your customers are already paying attention.

The companies with the strongest distribution position are the ones that started building it early and kept investing when it looked unsexy. Newsletter audiences built over a decade. Sales relationships maintained through quarters where they did not pay off. Communities where the trust is genuine because the company has been in them long enough to have history. These positions compound at a rate set by the relationship, not by the model. AI raises the cost of competing on this dimension. It does not let new entrants skip it.

### The exposed archetype

The exposed archetype is the company whose entire competitive position is "we build it better." For most companies that built their identity around execution quality, the moat is dissolving in real time and the leadership team has not noticed. Build quality is converging across the industry as AI raises the floor. The company that was three years ahead on execution is now six months ahead, and the gap is closing every quarter.

The middle-of-the-chain investments — the ones that look like efficiency, productivity, and operational excellence — are the ones AI is eating. Doubling down on them is doubling down on a position that is depreciating.

### The new investment shape

The companies winning the next decade are running a different shape of investment. Heavy at the why end. Heavy at the distribution end. Light in the middle, with AI absorbing the work that used to require a layer of people.

```
   investment shape, 2018:        investment shape, 2026:

   why ▓▓                         why ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓
   middle ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓              middle ▓▓
   distribution ▓▓▓▓               distribution ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓
```

Most companies are still running the 2018 shape. The cost of running it is rising, because the middle is the part AI is making cheap, and a heavy investment in a part that is getting cheap looks like efficiency on a spreadsheet and like a slow erosion of moat in reality.

### The diagnostic

The diagnostic for any business right now is to trace the chain from "why this exists" to "how this reaches the user" and ask where the compounding is actually happening. If the answer is at the why end (sharper taste, sharper judgment, sharper articulation than the market) or at the distribution end (channels and relationships that are getting harder to replicate), the moat is real. If the answer is somewhere in the middle — better internal tooling, faster execution, more efficient ops — the moat is decaying faster than the leadership team realises.

The companies that figured this out have already moved their investment to the bookends. The ones that have not are still hiring to make the middle better. The first group is going to look enormously more durable in three years. The second group is going to wonder what happened.
