The most honest reading of grindset is not that it produces worse work. It is a signal, and the signal works because nobody can see anything else.

Most knowledge work is illegible. Nobody outside your team can tell whether the deck was good. Nobody outside the cap table can tell whether the strategy was sound. Judgment is private. Effort is public. That asymmetry is not incidental to grindset. It is the whole reason grindset exists.

### Effort is the cheapest credential

In any market where the output is hard to evaluate, actors substitute easier metrics. Hours. Outputs shipped. Visible discomfort. The 4am alarm is not productivity.

Signalling.

The cheapest credential available to anyone who cannot show the more expensive one, which is taste.

The reason it works as a signal is that the cost is real. Sleep deprivation. Missed weekends. The bloodwork panel that says you are running too hot. Costly signals carry information, because nobody fakes a cost they do not have to pay. That is the part the grindset class understands implicitly and never says out loud. The cost is the credibility.

<div class="pull-quote">Effort is public. Judgment is private. That is the whole game.</div>

Grindset is loudest in industries where the bar for taste is high and the legibility of taste is low. Founders. Consultants. Investors. Bankers. The places where everyone knows judgment matters and nobody has a clean way to measure it from the outside. So the credential becomes hours. The credential becomes intensity. The credential becomes the screenshot of the inbox at zero, the calendar full of meetings, the LinkedIn post about the morning routine. The credential is anything that costs you something the next person is not paying.

### What the signal actually costs

The catch is who pays. The signal is socially valuable. The cost is privately catastrophic.

The grindset class is more anxious, more sleep-deprived, and more medicated than the generation before it. Cortisol elevated. SSRIs prescribed. GLP-1s repurposed as work accessories. The Whoop scores trending in the wrong direction year on year. The sleep tracker, the very tool of the optimisation cult, telling you the optimisation is failing, and the answer always being more optimisation. The body keeps the receipts. The signal does not.

This is not the same dynamic as a CEO pushing a winning team, or a coach asking more of an elite athlete. That is a different game, with a different accountability structure, and the person doing the pushing has line of sight into whether the push is working. Grindset is not someone being coached against a clear standard. It is someone running themselves into the ground because effort is the only signal they have left to send and they cannot afford to stop sending it.

The exposed archetype is the operator without taste or acumen using volume as a credential. Visible everywhere. Loudest in their thirties. Increasingly hard to distinguish from a person actually doing the work, until the work has to ship and the difference is finally legible.

### The signal is breaking

The arbitrage that built grindset is closing. The reason is unglamorous. Judgment is becoming legible faster than grindset's defenders can adapt to it.

AI is the proximate cause. When two people start with the same brief, the same data, and the same access to a model, the difference between their outputs is not hours. It is taste. And taste is becoming visible precisely because the inputs are being held constant. The deck either works or it does not. The strategy either compounds or it does not. The hiring decision either ages well or it does not, and the timeline on "ages well" is shrinking from years to months as feedback loops compress.

This is the part the 4am alarm class does not see coming. They built a career on a signal that was valuable because judgment was private. The signal stops working when judgment goes public. The cold plunge will keep working as a personal habit. It will stop working as a career credential. The same is true of the inbox at zero, the meeting count, the bragged-about hours.

<div class="pull-quote">The grindset trade was always temporary. It just took longer than expected for the trade to close.</div>

Grindset was never a productivity ideology. It was an information-asymmetry trade dressed up in masculine language about discipline. The trade is closing on its own. The people who built their careers on it are about to discover what their work actually looked like underneath, once the effort signal stops carrying the weight it used to. That bill is being paid in cortisol and credentialism. The second one is harder to recover from than the first.
